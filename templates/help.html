If you're new to ChronoWave and need help getting started, you've come to the right place. We've made it easy to help
yourself with our step-by-step guide which provides helpful guidelines and an easy walkthrough of how to upload you data,
analyze it, and visualize your results.

Step 1. Navigate to the Upload Data tab.
Step 2. Select the file you want to upload by clicking on the "Choose File" button. ChronoWave supports a wide range of file formats, including CSV, Exel, JSON, and txt, so you can upload your data in the format that best suits your needs.
Step 3. Once you're satisfied with you time series data, click the "Upload" button to upload you data to ChronoWave.

Step 1. Navigate to the Upload Data tab.
Step 2. Select the file you want to upload by clicking on the "Choose File" button. ChronoWave supports a wide range of file formats, including CSV, Exel, JSON, and txt, so you can upload your data in the format that best suits your needs.
Step 3. Click the "Upload" button to upload your data set to ChronoWave.
Step 4. Wait for ChronoWave to process your data set. This may take a few seconds or minutes, depending on the size of your data set.

Step 1. Navigate to the Dowload Data tab.
Step 2. Select the time series data set you want to download from the list of available data sets.
Step 3. Choose the desired output format from the dropdown menu, it can be CSV, Exel, JSON, or txt.
Step 4. Click the "Download" button to initiate the download of your time series data set in the chosen format.
Step 5. Wait for the download to complete. This may take a few seconds or minutes, depending on the size of your data set.
Step 6. Open the downloaded file in your preferred software, such as Microsoft Exel, Google Sheets, or a text editor.

Step 1. Navigate to the Performance Metrics tab on the Chronowavw website.
Step 2. Select the time series data set you want to view from the list of available data sets.
Step 3. Choose the desired performance metric from the dropdown menu. This will generate a plot showing the performance metric over time.
Step 4. If you are satisfied with the plot, you can download it by clicking the "Download" button. This will save the plot in a high-resolution image format, such as PNG or SVG.
Step 5. If you want to download the raw time series data set, click on the "Download Data" button. This will download the data set in the format your choice (CSV, Exel, JSON, or txt).
